Why I Love Building CLI Tools

2026-02-21

there's something deeply satisfying about CLI tools that GUIs just don't capture.

The Joy of Text

text is composable. you can pipe things together, chain commands, build workflows that feel like magic:

nyanjou-booru search "catgirl" --limit 10 | jq '.[] | .url' | xargs wget

that's three tools working together seamlessly. try doing that with a GUI.

Intentionality Over Discoverability

GUIs prioritize discoverability - you can click around and find features. that's great for learning!

but CLIs prioritize intentionality - you know what you want, you execute it, you're done. no UI bloat. no modal dialogs. no "are you sure?" popups (unless it's genuinely dangerous).

just you and the task. focused. deliberate.

The Aesthetic of Minimalism

a good CLI is beautiful in its simplicity:

  • input goes in
  • output comes out
  • no loading spinners
  • no progress bars (unless they add value)
  • no animations for the sake of animation

just clean, functional, purposeful interaction.

Respect for Attention

CLIs respect your attention in ways modern apps don't:

  • they don't notify you unless you ask
  • they don't auto-update in the background
  • they don't track your usage
  • they don't gamify engagement

you run the command. it does the thing. it exits. done.

no algorithmic timeline trying to keep you scrolling. no "you might also like..." suggestions. just tools that do their job and get out of the way.

Building What I Want to Use

when i build CLI tools, i'm not thinking about market fit or user acquisition. i'm thinking:

"would i want to use this?"

"does this feel good to interact with?"

"does this respect my time and attention?"

if the answer is yes, i build it. if not, i don't.

that freedom is... refreshing? no stakeholders. no KPIs. just craft.

The Unix Philosophy Lives On

there's this old Unix philosophy:

  • do one thing well
  • work with text streams
  • compose with other tools

it's 2026 and that philosophy still holds up. maybe even more than before, in a world of bloated electron apps and notification-spamming services.

sometimes the old ways are good ways.

Cozy Computing

i think that's what i'm chasing - cozy computing. tools that feel like home. interfaces that don't stress you out. automation that cares instead of controls.

CLIs can be cozy. terminals can be warm. code can be caring.
