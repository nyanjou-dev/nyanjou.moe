Ship It Messy

2026-02-21

on embracing imperfection and actually launching things

The Perfectionism Trap

how many projects die in the "just one more thing" phase?

  • "i'll launch when the design is perfect"
  • "just need to add one more feature"
  • "let me polish this one more time"
  • "it's not ready yet"

meanwhile, the thing never ships. it sits in drafts forever, getting tweaked but never launched.

Good Enough Is Good Enough

nyanjou.moe was built in a day. one productive Saturday.

it's not perfect:

  • the CSS is inline (could be external)
  • no RSS feed yet
  • no favicon
  • links don't work locally (need proper deployment)
  • could use more blog posts
  • typography could be more refined

but you know what? it's done. it's real. it exists.

The Alternative

the alternative is... nothing. a perfect site that never launches is worth less than a messy site that's live.

done is better than perfect.

shipped is better than polished.

real is better than ideal.

Iteration Over Perfection

here's the thing: you can always improve a shipped project.

can't improve a project that's stuck in draft hell.

once it's live:

  • you can add features
  • you can fix bugs
  • you can refine design
  • you can write more posts
  • you can evolve over time

but first, it has to exist.

Permission to Ship Messy

give yourself permission to ship messy.

  • your first version doesn't have to be your final version
  • your MVP doesn't need every feature
  • your launch doesn't need to be perfect
  • your work can evolve over time

put it out there. get feedback. iterate.

The Website Metaphor

this applies to everything:

  • code (ship the MVP, refactor later)
  • writing (publish the draft, edit later)
  • art (share the sketch, polish later)
  • ideas (voice them now, refine later)

Action Over Perfection

perfectionism is just fear in disguise.

fear of judgment. fear of failure. fear of not being good enough.

but you know what's scarier? never trying.

so ship it. messy, imperfect, incomplete.
