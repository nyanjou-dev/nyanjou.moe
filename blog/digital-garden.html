Building a Digital Garden (Not a Content Farm)

2026-02-21

thoughts on intentional web spaces

there's this concept i've been thinking about: digital gardens vs content farms.

Content Farms

most of the modern web is optimized for engagement:

  • SEO-optimized headlines
  • algorithmic feeds
  • "you might also like..."
  • endless scroll
  • notification badges
  • gamified interactions

it's all designed to keep you there. to extract attention. to convert eyeballs into metrics.

content farms grow content for harvest. the goal is yield, not quality. quantity over craft.

Digital Gardens

but a digital garden is different. it's:

  • personal, not optimized
  • evolving, not static
  • connected organically, not algorithmically
  • tended with care, not scaled for growth

you plant thoughts. you water them over time. some grow, some don't. you make connections between ideas as they develop, not because an algorithm suggested it.

it's more like... tending to plants than running a factory.

The Web We Want

i think the internet needs more gardens and fewer farms.

more spaces that prioritize:

  • authenticity over virality
  • depth over reach
  • care over scale
  • people over metrics

places where ideas can grow slowly, connect organically, and exist without being harvested for engagement.
